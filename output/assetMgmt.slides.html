$\bf{\mu / \sigma}$ dogma

First two moments are sufficient to derive the utility of any given return distribution.

$\bf{\mu / \sigma}$ deficiencies

σ inadequate to capture all kinds of risk:

  • as symmetric measure unaffected by reflection at mean value

Alternative measure: VaR

  • VaR: value exceeded with given probability $\bf{\alpha}$

Benefits

  • focusing on downside risk

 ⇒  more appropriate for asymmetric return distributions

  • more meaningful: defining risk aversion in terms of VaR more intuitive to private investors (?)
  • aligned with financial regulation

Nobody's perfect

  • values beyond VaR are not taken into account

 ⇒  reduction to single value causes loss of information

  • VaR more difficult to evaluate
